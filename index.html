About Me

Tennessee native, former chef, and software developer, I hope to contribute to the development community while continuing to learn and improve my skills.

I have always loved learning and mastering new skills, which is what originally drew me to cooking as a profession. I moved to San Francisco and attended culinary school at City College of San Francisco. After six years in the food industry, I had worked my way up to executive chef, but I decided it was time for a change.

I moved back home to attend Nashville Software School and study full stack web development. I've never enjoyed learning a new skill as much as I do now. Whether it's new technologies to learn, more effecient ways to solve problems, or new syntax/techniques to use, there is always more to learn and improve on in a community driven industry.

The ability to explore how things work, look up alternative solutions to problems, have access to tutorials and learning materials, and receive help from the community at large are things that don't exist in most other industries, and it's what drives me to be the best developer that I can be.

Flavorize

Flavorize is a recipe building app designed for use by both professional chefs and at home cooks. Users can view recipes created by others, create their own recipes, get food pairing suggestions based what ingredients they use, and get nutritional information on both a per recipe and per serving level. Users can also upload images of their recipes, as well as edit or delete their own recipes.

Technologies used: AngularJS, Bootstrap, Firebase, Foodpairing and National Nutrient Database APIs, CSS, HTML, Javascript

  • Live Demo: corytohill.github.io/flavorize/
  • View on GitHub: github.com/CoryTohill/flavorize

BirdyBoard

BirdyBoard is a single line message board application built in Python. Users can register profiles, select which profile they want to log in as, view and send both public and private messages.

Technologies used: Python

  • View on GitHub: github.com/CoryTohill/birdyboard

Chatty!

Chatty is a message board application. It was given as a Nashville Software School group assignment and was our first introduction to reading information from JSON files. Users are presented with pre-written messages when the app first loads. They can create their own messages, delete individual messages, delete all messages, and select between different style options.

Technologies used: HTML, CSS, jQuery, Javascript, Gulp, and JSON

  • Live Demo: corytohill.github.io/spa-group-chatty-cds/
  • View on GitHub: github.com/CoryTohill/spa-group-chatty-cds
